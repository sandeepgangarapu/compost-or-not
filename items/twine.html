Can I Compost Twine?

Yes

Twine can be composted.

What to Know

  • Twine can be composted, but only if it's made from natural fibers like cotton, jute, or hemp
  • Synthetic twine (nylon, polyester) should not be composted
  • Natural twine will decompose slowly, so it's best to cut it into smaller pieces

Tips

  • Cut natural twine into 4-6 inch pieces before adding to compost
  • Spread it out in the pile rather than clumping it together
  • Avoid twine treated with preservatives or dyes
  • If unsure about the material, it's better to err on the side of caution and not compost it
