Can I Compost Garlic skins?

Yes

Garlic skins can be composted.

What to Know

  • Garlic skins are excellent for composting
  • They're a 'green' material, rich in nitrogen
  • They break down quickly and add valuable nutrients to your compost
  • Garlic skins also have natural antifungal properties that can benefit your compost pile

Tips

  • Mix garlic skins with other compost materials to avoid clumping
  • If you're adding a large quantity, consider shredding them first to speed up decomposition
  • The strong odor may initially attract pests, so bury the skins in the center of your compost pile
