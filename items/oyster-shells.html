Can I Compost Oyster shells?

Yes

Oyster shells can be composted.

What to Know

  • Oyster shells are slow to decompose and are rich in calcium
  • They can help balance pH in acidic soils and provide essential minerals
  • Shells should be cleaned and crushed before adding to compost

Tips

  • Crush shells into small pieces to speed up decomposition
  • Use sparingly as they decompose slowly
  • Consider using as a soil amendment directly in garden beds for calcium-loving plants like tomatoes
