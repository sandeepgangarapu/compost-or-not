Can I Compost Pine needles?

Yes

Pine needles can be composted.

What to Know

  • Pine needles can be composted, but they decompose slowly and are acidic
  • They're considered a 'brown' material in composting terms, providing carbon to the compost pile
  • Due to their waxy coating and acidic nature, they take longer to break down than other materials

Tips

  • Shred or chop pine needles before adding to speed up decomposition
  • Mix with 'green' materials like grass clippings or food scraps to balance the carbon-to-nitrogen ratio
  • Use pine needles in moderation, as too many can make your compost overly acidic
  • Consider using them as mulch around acid-loving plants as an alternative to composting
