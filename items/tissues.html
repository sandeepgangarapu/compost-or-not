Can I Compost Tissues?

Yes

Tissues can be composted.

What to Know

  • Tissues are generally compostable as they are made from paper, which is a carbon-rich 'brown' material
  • However, only compost plain, unscented, and unbleached tissues
  • Avoid tissues with lotion, antibacterial agents, or synthetic fibers

Tips

  • Tear tissues into smaller pieces to speed up decomposition
  • Mix with 'green' materials like food scraps for balanced compost
  • If tissues are used for wiping noses during illness, it's best to discard them in the trash to prevent spreading pathogens in your compost
